We hold weekly Anime showings, which encourage you to meet fellow Anime fans and Japanophiles.  Anyone can come out to our events from the simply curious, to the serious Anime fans. <div style="text-align: left;">

<p><span class="news-date">2010/09/13</span> <span class="news-title">End of an Era</span> - It is with great regret that I announce the closure of SOAP. There are many reasons for this, which I will not go into. I do want to make it known that I do not regret one day of running this club. It has been an honour and a pleasure to serve the many fans and businesses in the Ottawa region. I hope and believe that the fandom will continue for many more years.</p>
<p>This closure affects only our events. Our web sites (Ottawa-Anime.Org, SOAP.Ottawa-Anime.Org and all its affiliates) will continue to be hosted here. The forums will continue to remain open for the Ottawa fandom to use and enjoy as an online community.</p>
<p>Thank you once again everybody for eleven years of wonderful memories.</p>
<p><b>Curtis Ireland</b> (Co-Chair or SOAP)</p>

		<h2><strong>Welcome to Mu Epsilon Kappa!</strong></h2>
		<p></p>
		<h3>What do we do?</h3>
		<p>Watch anime! That's all we do, really. Wednesdays 7-10 and Saturdays 6-10, we watch anime. I'm also taking suggestions for other activities like a video game Saturday or DnD group. Just send me an e-mail and we'll talk!</p>
		<h3>Where do you watch anime?</h3>
		<p>The Student Union Building, usually. Unfortunately, due to university rules, we can't book more than three meetings at a time, so keep your eyes on the emails or the website.</p>
		<h3>How do you decide what to watch?</h3>
		<p>The first two meetings of the semester are dedicated to showcasing the new anime for the semester. We encourage everyone to bring in shows that fit the guidelines! We watch the first episode of each series and then vote on what to watch for the rest of the semester! We do our best to finish off every series, within reason, so you won't be left hanging.</p>
		<h3>What are the guidelines for the shows to bring in?</h3>
		<p>If a show is running in Japan and it is being fansubbed, bring the fansubs in. If a show has been licensed in America, but has not been released on DVD and you have fansubs, bring in the fansubs. If the show has been licensed in America and is out on DVD, bring the DVD. Apart from that, make sure there's no blatant sex. Violence and cursing are okay, but sex scenes aren't. If it can't be shown on FX, it can't be shown in anime club. Also, if a show is on American TV and everyone has seen it (Naruto, Bleach, Cowboy Bebop), refrain from bringing it in. We want to showcase stuff not everyone has seen.</p>
		<h3>What if I have a short series or a movie?</h3>
		<p>Great! On Saturdays, for the last hour, we usually have happy hour. This means you can bring in your short series or movie and we'll watch it. The happy hour schedule will be determined once all the anime has been voted in and a schedule can be made up.</p>
		<p><i><b>What language do we watch anime in?</b></i></p>
		<p>Traditionally, we've only watched shows in Japanese with English subtitles, however we're trying out two shows in English this semester--Nerima Daikon Brothers and xxxHolic.</p>
		<h3>What do I have to do to join?</h3>
		<p>Nothing. Not a thing. All you have to do is show up. We don't take attendance, this isn't a class. No dues, no fees, no nothin'. Just a bunch of people who have a mutual interest hanging out and enjoying themselves.</p>
		<p><h2>Remember:</h2> All series brought to MEK that are licensed in America must be on their original DVDs.</p>
		<p>&nbsp;</p>
	</div>

		<h4>About us</h4>
		<p>Mu Epsilon Kappa Anime (aka "MEK") is a Japanese animation fan club at Texas Tech University.  Our purpose is to provide a safe, comfortable environment where anime fans from all over can come together and watch anime.  In addition to this, MEK also wishes to promote world peace through the sharing of popular culture.</p>

						<h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://www.emeraldtokyo.com/?p=729">EAC is going Dark on Jan 18th</a></h2>
<span class="post-author">by <a href="http://www.emeraldtokyo.com/?author=1">EmeraldTokyo</a></span>
<span class="posted-on">on&nbsp;</span><span class="post-date">January 15, 2012</span>
<span class="posted-at">at&nbsp;</span><span class="post-time">6:01 pm</span>
<div class="post-cat">Posted In: <a href="http://www.emeraldtokyo.com/?cat=1" title="View all posts in Website News" rel="category">Website News</a></div>
					</div>
				</div>
				<div class="clear"></div>
				<div class="entry">
					<p>Emerald Angel Entertainment/Comics and all our related pages will be going dark on January 18th in protest of SOPA.</p>
<p>I understand why piracy is a problem. but, this bill is poorly written and threatens all of the internet on various levels. The number one reason is, if a complaint is filed against any website for infringing copyrights &#8230; there&#8217;s no need of any proof! The website under scrutiny will be shut down. PERIOD! This means, any little jerk off with a chip on their shoulder can run around and make false claims if they want and there&#8217;s nothing in place to stop them.</p>
<p>The number two reason this is a horrible bill is because all fan artists out there will no longer be able to sketch and post fan art of their favorite Marvel, DC, Disney, or whatever characters without permission from the original creators. The websites the fan art is posted on will be shut down &#8230; not just the offending piece of art. How ridiculous is this?</p>
<p>Of course, I&#8217;ll have to yank all my fan art if this passes. However, once my comic series are established, I&#8217;ll post a Public Notice on a page that automatically gives written permission to ALL my fans to draw EAC characters as long as they don&#8217;t sell or profit from them. If any company out there like Marvel, DC, or whatever respects their fans, they would do the same thing.</p>
<p>Of course, SOPA needs to go down in flames and the entire bill needs to be rewritten. I&#8217;d like to protect my copyrights, but not at the extent of throwing a 5 year old in jail for sticking a scribble of one of my characters on their fridge. I know it&#8217;s probably not THAT bad &#8230; but it&#8217;s close!</p>
<p>So, don&#8217;t be surprised if we are missing in action on the 18th. We aren&#8217;t the only ones going black either. Here&#8217;s a list of websites going dark on the 18th on NLB&#8217;s website. The Cheezburger Network and Newgrounds are among those going dark. <a href="http://www.nlb-creations.com/2012/01/13/websites-confirmed-for-the-jan-18-blackout-protesting-sopa/" title="Websites Confirmed for the Jan 18 Blackout Protesting SOPA | NLB Creations" target="_blank">Websites Confirmed for the Jan 18 Blackout Protesting SOPA | NLB Creations</a></p>
<p>Luv,<br />
Chrissy.</p>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>*squeals with excitement* I&#8217;m up at 1AM and I&#8217;m OH SO HAPPY! HAP HAP HAPPY!</p>
<p>Ok, I&#8217;ll calm down and tell you why. I have been working non-stop this week on 3D Backgrounds for my comic series. Yeah, I&#8217;m better at modeling than drawing buildings in perspective. Plus, it takes less time to model my stuff and take screenshots for backgrounds. That way once the model is finished, I just have to render the scene for the background, toss my characters INTO this nice 3D perspective and BING! Next scene!</p>
<p>Now, I don&#8217;t have a lot of money &#8230; in fact &#8230; I&#8217;m as poor as you expect a dirt poor artist to get. I haggle at Goodwill that&#8217;s how poor I am right now.</p>
<p>Anyway, I digressed. The fact of the matter is a lot of 3D Programs out there sure aren&#8217;t cheap. Unless you&#8217;re a hard core penny pincher and you sniff out the free stuff. So, not long ago I ran into Google Sketchup. The regular version is free &#8230; the pro version costs a little. But, right now I only need the free version.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s insanely easy to model with &#8230; beats Studio Max in how quick it is to model with and I like that. I hated Max in school. In fact I hated it so much I swore off 3D modeling as any part of my future whatsoever. Enter &#8230; Sketchup &#8230;</p>
<p>I saw a simple tutorial while messing around on YouTube and my jaw dropped. That easy huh? Then saw some tutorials about how to export the models as line art for comic book backgrounds. I was intrigued seeing as how drawing backgrounds was my weak point and I got the program. I messed with it a little bit. Did a few models. Made a 3D Tardis from Dr. Who as a kinda gift for Mark. <img src='http://www.emeraldtokyo.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_biggrin.gif' alt=':D' class='wp-smiley' /> </p>
<p>See below</p>
<p><img src="http://fc06.deviantart.net/fs71/i/2011/217/2/9/3d_tardis_finished_by_emeraldtokyo-d45l3g8.png" alt="3D Tardis on DA" /></p>
<p>And, well then I got so busy with other things and got down on myself &#8230; (Yes, even with my ego I do self loath at times) and kinda quit for awhile. But, for the heck of it I started modeling Chrissy&#8217;s castle for my comics series. I figured, why not? Got so slap happy with it I decided, why just export it as line art to color later? This stuff looks so good I can use it with the colors, shading, shadows, and textures! It&#8217;s awesome! And, it&#8217;d save a lot of time too. Especially since I don&#8217;t wanna pay a colorist to help with line art. Since no one was helping me anyway even if I flashed cash at them. Ya jerks.</p>
<p>Anyway, I&#8217;ve been having a blast. I have the castle almost complete. It&#8217;s looking nice. Mark says it&#8217;s beautiful. As a perfectionist I don&#8217;t think I can say that til I&#8217;m done with it. I was working on some of the indoor stuff when I realized &#8230; *GASP* &#8230; SketchuTrying URL http://www.neoanime.org/...
p does not have something Max has. Something vital to my backgrounds &#8230; lights! There&#8217;s no light sources! Only the sun for the shadows. I saw a few tutorials on how to prevent the ceiling of the model from casting a shadow to let the sunlight in &#8230; but that doesn&#8217;t help me with lamps, light-bulbs, campfires, candles, magic spells blasting everything within a ten mile radius, ya know &#8230; that sort of thing. So, I did some more searching &#8230; and found a program that works with Sketchup that costs &#8230; $800 bucks! Yeah sure, there&#8217;s a 30 day trial version &#8230; and I could pirate &#8230; but why risk my freedom? I am now up at 1AM doing research into trying to find something free that does what I need. And, via the Ruby Scripts with Sketchup I FOUND IT! A free ray-tracing rendering program called POV-Ray! Oh boy, the learning curve! Took me all of an hour to figure this one out. I&#8217;m ashamed. Shoulda been instant &#8230; but I am tired &#8230; yeah I&#8217;ll blame it on that.</p>
<p>Anyway, there&#8217;s a Ruby script that works with POV-Ray called SU2POV. So I downloaded the ruby script and after being frustrated for some time because it didn&#8217;t set up in the folder correctly so it didn&#8217;t even show in the plugin&#8217;s drop down menu &#8230; ergh &#8230; I got it to work. Downloaded and installed POV-Ray and it&#8217;s BEAUTIFUL! I got it working after a little bit of tutorials reading and it renders night scenes with reflections and everything! I&#8217;m so excited now I can&#8217;t sleep!</p>
<p>I&#8217;m going to be up all night long working on this! Eeeeeeee!!!</p>
<p>Luv,<br />
Chrissy.</p>
